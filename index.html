Brain Aneurysm Awareness

Understanding Symptoms, Risks, and Real Stories

What is a Brain Aneurysm?

A brain aneurysm is a bulge or ballooning in a blood vessel in the brain that can leak or rupture, causing bleeding into the brain (hemorrhagic stroke). It can develop without symptoms and become life-threatening if it ruptures.

Common Symptoms

  • Sudden severe headache (often described as "the worst headache of your life")
  • Nausea or vomiting
  • Stiff neck
  • Blurred or double vision
  • Sensitivity to light
  • Seizure
  • Loss of consciousness
  • Confusion or trouble concentrating

My Personal Experience

After being taken to the emergency room, doctors performed a CAT scan that was negative. Due to my symptoms, the doctor suspected possible meningitis. Upon family consent to perform a spinal tap, he discovered blood and immediately knew I was bleeding on the brain.

As a result, I was transported to a larger, more equipped facility. Upon arrival, I was immediately taken for an angiogram which showed a blurry image of uncertainty. Days later, a second scan was much clearer and showed the bleed.

The following morning, I underwent an 8+ hour brain surgery called a craniotomy to insert an aneurysm clip to secure the area.

Prevention & Awareness

Know the warning signs and risk factors: smoking, high blood pressure, genetics, drug use, and trauma. If you suspect symptoms, seek medical attention immediately.
